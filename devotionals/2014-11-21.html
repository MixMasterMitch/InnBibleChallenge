Life of Exile: Returning and Rebuilding

Today's reading assignment is Ezra 1:1-8, 8:15-36, 9:1-4 and Nehemiah 2, 13. As you read, pay attention to the focus on building a nation with God at the center and the expectations placed on obedience and life practice to ensure the goal.

King Cyrus of the Persian Empire eventually allows the exiles to return to Judah and gives them permission and support to rebuild a temple to God, the walls of Jerusalem, as well as the community.

Reflection Questions

Why would reforms toward a more “godly” nation be such a focus for the returning exiles? What’s dangerous about such strict spiritual formation?

Questions To Consider Everyday

  • In what ways does this story connect with the overall narrative of scripture as I know it?
  • What side of the character of God do I see displayed in this story?
  • How is the character of God I see lived out in Jesus' life/Where is Jesus in the story?
  • In what ways does this story connect with my story?

Connection to Your Faith

What most forms your identity as a follower of Jesus? Is it what you do or what God does within you?

New Testament Bonus

Read Luke 19:1-10. How is Jesus encouragement to include the outsider different from what we see in Ezra & Nehemiah?
