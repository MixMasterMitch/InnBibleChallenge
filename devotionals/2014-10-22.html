Week 2: The Law and The Land

Freedom From Slavery

This Exodus from Egypt is one of two cataclysmic, defining events in the life of Israel throughout the Old Testament. It is through this Exodus that Israel sees itself and continues to define itself even today. This is not a history lesson read about the past but is told as a story that is continuing to happen right now because we always have new pharaohs, we always have new circumstances of slavery.

The themes of God come through loudly in the Exodus. First, it is a story of deliverance from slavery. It demonstrates in a world divided into haves and have-nots, God is on the side of the have-nots. Israel has a God of freedom from that which binds. Second, it is a story of provision, the Israelites wander in the wilderness for 40 years and God provides sustenance with no visible source.
