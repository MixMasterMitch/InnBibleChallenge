Rise of the prophets to the Northern Kingdom (Israel)

Israel & Judah spent little time paying attention to God, and many prophets spoke to Israel & Judah on God’s behalf to remind them of the covenant of promise, deliverance and provision, the covenant that they had with their God who brought them out of the land of Egypt. Instead of acknowledging God and living into their calling as God’s people, Israel continued to invest in the things and cultures of the world around them, ignoring God, the prophets, and the potential threats of outside invaders.

There are several prophetic books in the Old Testament, and they are typically focused on the themes of Judgment (by God on Israel & Judah), Loss (of everything Israel & Judah knew and thought), and finally Hope (redemption out of destruction).

Today's reading assignment is Hosea 1; 2; 11:1-11 Amos 4; 5. As you read, pay attention to the harsh descriptions that God uses in reference to Israel, and the hurt and feelings of betrayal apparent in God’s words.

Reflection Questions

What does it say about God that God experiences such pain and jealousy when those God loves are so ambivalent about a covenant relationship?

Questions To Consider Everyday

  • In what ways does this story connect with the overall narrative of scripture as I know it?
  • What side of the character of God do I see displayed in this story?
  • How is the character of God I see lived out in Jesus' life/Where is Jesus in the story?
  • In what ways does this story connect with my story?

Connection to Your Faith

How does the brokenness of the relationship God has with Israel resonate in your own life and faith journey?

New Testament Bonus
