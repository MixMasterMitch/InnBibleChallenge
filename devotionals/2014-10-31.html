The Calling of David

Today's reading assignment is 1 Samuel 16:1-13 and 17:48-18:16, 2 Samuel 5:1-25 and 7. As you read, pay attention to the characteristics of David that most demonstrate the characteristics of God and result in David symbolizing Israel’s ideal king.

Additional Info

The authority of King Saul is challenged and many Israelites begin to support David instead of Saul after David kills the giant Philistine, Goliath. Israel begins to become a kingdom and has the most prosperous time in its history under King David—the king after God’s own heart—and his son, King Solomon, which lasted about 100 years. The capital, Jerusalem, was a thriving city, God was the center of life for Israel, and Solomon completed a temple where God could dwell with his chosen people, the Israelites. For a short time, Israel was the community it saw itself as being: “The people called by God for the sake of the world.”

Reflection Questions

What is upheld in David’s character to show he is a man after God’s own heart?

Questions To Consider Everyday

  • In what ways does this story connect with the overall narrative of scripture as I know it?
  • What side of the character of God do I see displayed in this story?
  • How is the character of God I see lived out in Jesus' life/Where is Jesus in the story?
  • In what ways does this story connect with my story?

Connection to Your Faith

Would you describe yourself as a person after God’s own heart?

What characteristics in you show God’s image to the world?

New Testament Bonus

Read Matthew 2:1-2 and 27:11-14. How does Jesus’ life embody “kingship” as God wants us to understand it?
