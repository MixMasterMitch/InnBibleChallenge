Becoming a Community and a Nation

Today's reading assignment is Judges 1:6-23, 3, 4, 7. As you read, pay attention to the chaos that seems to be at the forefront of Israel’s experience as it moves into the land and the cycle of sin that Israel falls into in its relationship with God.

The additional information section might be more applicable to read before the scripture.

Reflection Questions

How can God be present in this turbulent period in Israel’s history of forging a nation?

Questions To Consider Everyday

  • In what ways does this story connect with the overall narrative of scripture as I know it?
  • What side of the character of God do I see displayed in this story?
  • How is the character of God I see lived out in Jesus' life/Where is Jesus in the story?
  • In what ways does this story connect with my story?

Connection to Your Faith

Is there anything in this part of Israel’s history you find difficult to reconcile with your own faith and understanding of God? How so?

Additional Info

When Israel reaches the promised land and moves into it, Israel divides up the land amongst the 12 tribes, which were the descendants of the 12 sons of Jacob (described in Joshua). The tribes begin the difficult process of being God’s people (a community set apart with a specific covenant) amongst other peoples and cultures. Outside cultures and religions influence the people of Israel heavily, they forget their God and choose other Gods. Israel falls into a cycle of sin, oppression by outside invaders, repentance for denying God, and deliverance by a “Judge,” or a leader, from within Israel’s community.
