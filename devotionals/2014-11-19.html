Week 6: A New Community Begins

Judah falls to the Babylonian empire in 587 BC. The second cataclysmic, defining event for the Israelites was the exile to Babylon. Much of the Old Testament is written to come to terms with this date when Babylon destroyed Jerusalem and the temple, the place where God was supposed to dwell with them. After the exile happens, many prophets take the old stories of promise, deliverance, provision and covenant that they could remember (Genesis and Exodus) and ask— given that, how are we to understand this destruction? Where is God? How could our God abandon us? It was a defining loss, the loss of everything after too long of living in an illusion.

Many Israelites from Judah are taken captive and move into exile in Babylon. Some Old Testament books provide us with accounts of what it was like to live in Exile amongst a foreign culture.

Life of Exile: Obedience

Today's reading assignment is Daniel 1, 2, and 6. As you read, pay attention to the faithfulness of God’s people even when they are far from their home and tossed in a foreign, hostile experience.

Reflection Questions

How does discipline and obedience offer us freedom?

Questions To Consider Everyday

  • In what ways does this story connect with the overall narrative of scripture as I know it?
  • What side of the character of God do I see displayed in this story?
  • How is the character of God I see lived out in Jesus' life/Where is Jesus in the story?
  • In what ways does this story connect with my story?

Connection to Your Faith

Does your obedience result from the love Jesus has for you or from your need to “earn” the love Jesus has for you? How can you see the difference?

New Testament Bonus

Read Luke 17:20-21. Daniel interpreting the dream references a future kingdom. What does Jesus say about this future kingdom?
