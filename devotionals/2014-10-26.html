Promised Land

Today's reading assignment is Joshua 1, 6, 8, 11:16-23. As you read, pay attention to: As Israel moves into the promised land, they are nervous about the cultures they will face, but God promises to go before them.

Reflection Questions

How do you see God present in the midst of Israel conquering those in the promised land? Does it make you uneasy? Why or why not?

Questions To Consider Everyday

  • In what ways does this story connect with the overall narrative of scripture as I know it?
  • What side of the character of God do I see displayed in this story?
  • How is the character of God I see lived out in Jesus' life/Where is Jesus in the story?
  • In what ways does this story connect with my story?

Connection to Your Faith

When have you seen God’s promises come to fruition in your own life and faith?

Additional Info

Under Joshua's lead, the Israelites traveled north into modern day Jordan. At the north side of the Dead Sea, they traveled west to Jericho. Their way was blocked by the Jordan River, which God miraculously blocked to allow passage. They camped near Gilgal between marches around Jericho.

Jericho was rebuilt and is a city of the West Bank.

Blog post The Walls of Jericho provides some very interesting information about the miraculous destruction of Jericho.

New Testament Bonus

Read Luke 17:20-21. What does the “promised land” look like with Jesus and the New Testament witness?
