Wandering and Grumbling

Today's reading assignment is Numbers 11 and 14. As you read, pay attention to the expectations placed on God by the Israelites for what their life looks like as they wander in the desert and wait to enter the promised land.

Reflection Questions

Why would Israel long for Egypt even though when there, they were in slavery?

Questions To Consider Everyday

  • In what ways does this story connect with the overall narrative of scripture as I know it?
  • What side of the character of God do I see displayed in this story?
  • How is the character of God I see lived out in Jesus' life/Where is Jesus in the story?
  • In what ways does this story connect with my story?

Connection to Your Faith

In what ways is a life and lifestyle that does not reflect your faith in Jesus appealing to you? Why?

Additional Info

The traditional belief is that the Israelites wandered in the wilderness of northeast Sinai Peninsula. Newer evidence would strongly suggest that they wandered the wilderness in Midian (modern Saudi Arabia). Restrictions by the Saudi government make it difficult for archaeologists to find additional evidence.

The wilderness in Saudi Arabia or the Sinai Peninsula would have been unpleasant either way.
