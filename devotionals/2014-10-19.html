Joseph the Rejected Becomes a Savior

Today's reading assignment is Genesis 37, 39, and 41:41-43. As you read, pay attention to Joseph’s faithfulness in the midst of increasingly difficult circumstances and how God uses those circumstances for good.

Extra Information

Because of a drought in the land promised to Abraham, Jacob’s family (the people of Israel) move to Egypt for food. Joseph forgives his family in a beautiful scene of reconciliation (Genesis 45) Because of Joseph’s high ranking position in Egypt, the entire family eventually settles in Egypt, are fruitful and multiply, which poses a threat to Egyptian power. As a result, the Egyptian leader, Pharaoh, enslaves the Israelites for 400 years.

Reflection Questions

How does the life of Joseph reflect the idea of “blessed to be a blessing”?

Connection to Your Faith

Jesus talks about being “persecuted because of me.” In what ways does persecution strengthen your own faith and in what ways does it deter you from pursuing faith?

New Testament Bonus

Read Matthew 26:47-56. Like Joseph, Jesus was betrayed by a loved one. How are Joseph and Jesus’ lives similar and different?
