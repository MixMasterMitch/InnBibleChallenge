Week 4: The Kingdom Rises and Falls

Over time, Israel becomes a kingdom and has the most prosperous time in its history under King David—the king after God’s own heart—and his son, King Solomon. The capital, Jerusalem, was a thriving city, God was the center of life for Israel, and Solomon completed a temple where God could dwell with his chosen people Israel. For a short time, Israel was the community it saw itself as being: “The people called by God for the sake of the world.”

Israel Under King Solomon

Today's reading assignment is 1 Kings 1:28-2:12, 3:1-28, 6:1-13, and 11:1-13. As you read, pay attention to the prosperity of Israel under King Solomon and the ways in which he uses the kingship to demonstrate God’s blessings for Israel.

Reflection Questions

How did Solomon reflect God’s character in his kingship?

How did Solomon not reflect God’s character in his kingship?

Questions To Consider Everyday

  • In what ways does this story connect with the overall narrative of scripture as I know it?
  • What side of the character of God do I see displayed in this story?
  • How is the character of God I see lived out in Jesus' life/Where is Jesus in the story?
  • In what ways does this story connect with my story?

Connection to Your Faith

What would you ask God for if there were one characteristic of Jesus you could internalize and share with the world?

New Testament Bonus

Read John 2:12-25. What does the temple have to do with our Christian faith?
